# Launch phases (go-to-market)


## Phase 0 — Before the main marketing site

**Goal:** Check that **problem and message** work before we spend heavily on design.

**Do**

- Short **waitlist** or interest form: role (brand/creator), region, one-line value prop.
- **Direct outreach** to a short list of brands and creators we want in beta; a conversation beats an anonymous form entry.
- Test **headlines** (verified views, pay per view, FB/TikTok, payouts, etc.) and see what gets replies.

**Done when**

- We can state **who is in / out** for v1 (region, campaign size, creator tier) in concrete terms.
- Both sides show interest, **or** we have a clear plan to bring one side first (e.g. anchor brands).

**Watch out**

- Long waitlist, few real conversations → add **qualifying** questions or calls.
- Building features from loud feedback **before** MVP ships → we log ideas; we only build what blocks beta.

---

## Phase 1 — Marketing website

**Goal:** Explain the product in plain language; one clear **main action** on the page (waitlist, apply, or book a call).

**Do**

- Page order: **problem** → **how Arpify works (high level)** → **proof or founder story** → **that single main action**.
- Basic **SEO / sharing**: title, description, social preview, simple analytics.
- Short trust copy: verified views, what brands and creators should expect; link to Terms for detail.

**Done when**

- Signup flow is **steady enough** to feed Phase 2; visit quality and signup rate meet our bar.
- No mismatch between **site promises** and what our support tells people.

---

## Phase 2 — Closed beta (MVP live)

**Goal:** Prove the **full loop** with a **small beta group** we can support. We treat beta as where we **build case studies** (and optional creative assets) for **after beta**.

**Do**

- Pick brands and creators who can handle rough edges; include **variety** of campaign types if our model needs it. Where possible, we include partners who will allow **name, logo, quote** when things go well — we put **visibility** in beta terms **early**, not only at public launch.
- **High-touch support:** onboarding calls, clear **agreed response time** (e.g. “we reply within X hours in beta”).
- **Track where people stall:** fund, publish, connect social, submit, **reject**/review (minimum event list).
- Share [policies](06-policies-and-trust.md) with everyone in the beta; **no** surprise rules after they start.
- **Case studies from day one:** For each campaign, agree what **success** means (e.g. verified views, cost per 1k, time to first **submission**). Save **baseline vs outcome**, screenshots or **anonymized** dashboard **captures** if allowed, and short **interview questions** for brand (and creator if useful). Target **one-page** stories: problem → what they did on Arpify → outcome → quote — so Phase 4 can paste into site and decks.

**Done when**

- Everyone understands **deposits, who can reject submissions, and payouts** (some steps may still be manual).
- We have **themes** from interviews **and** **completion rates** for the main journey.
- We have **≥1 case study or win package** (metrics + permission to share) for marketing **after** beta.

**Watch out**

- The beta group **too big** too soon → support and fraud risk jump.
- Only **friend-and-family** campaigns → we don’t learn disputes, timing pressure, or real objections.
- Beta ends with **no asset we may publish** → we negotiate **data and publicity** up front and **write outcomes as we go**.

---

## Phase 3 — After beta (stabilize)

**Goal:** Turn beta feedback into **fixes** and **written step-by-step guides for ops** so the next wave doesn’t multiply incidents.

**Do**

- Group issues: fraud, UX confusion, payout edge cases, slow brand **review/reject**, creator onboarding — each has an owner and a target end state.
- **Finalize case studies:** edit, get legal/brand sign-off, ship the **formats** we need (one-pager, site block, short video) **before** large **sales and marketing outreach**.
- **Ops checklists / playbooks:** e.g. dispute steps, monthly payout review checklist, email templates for common failures.
- **Policy** updates where the product cannot fix it alone (retention wording, reject reasons).
- **Cut or delay** work that does not fix a top theme.

**Done when**

- **Launch checklist** exists: must-fix vs later, with owners.
- Support load per active user is **under control** as we add users in a controlled way.

---

## Phase 4 — Wider release and product work

**Goal:** Open the product in steps; ship what **removes blockers** to **repeat** use.

**Do**

- Open by segment, region, or invite; if metrics slip, **slow down or pause new invites** until we fix the cause.
- Put **Phase 2 case studies and quotes** on the site, in decks, and in **prospect outreach** so people see **proof**.
- Roadmap from **numbers we actually measure**: first funded campaign, first **payout batch confirmed**, second campaign, second submit, etc.
- **Partners** (agencies, creator groups) only after the core loop runs reliably.

**Done when**

- People **return** without us manually chasing **every new batch of users**.
- We can say clearly **what v2 will not do** (protect focus).

---

## Phase 5 — Scale

**Goal:** Grow **revenue and usage** with predictable operations and risk.

**Do**

- **Reliability:** rate limits, monitoring, incidents, data retention per trust needs.
- **Money:** reconciliation, chargebacks, tax and payout rules — process scales with volume.
- **Trust:** rule checks, sampling, appeals — balance **automation vs human review** using measured error rates.
- **Growth** must not break balance (e.g. many creators, few campaigns, or the opposite).

**Done when**

- **Cost and revenue per user**, and ops cost, are understood for the **next** big step in traffic (our definition).
- **Payments and platform policies** (TikTok/Meta, etc.) have a named owner and a **review schedule**.

---


## Meaning of “MVP” here

**MVP** = smallest version that completes: **fund → visible campaign → submit with locked stats → count by default, reject to exclude → monthly payout story** aligned with [business model](01-business-model.md). Marketing **before** MVP is fine; we shouldn’t promise **finished polish** before the loop works.

**One-line summary for slides:** demand → site → **closed beta (learn + case studies)** → stabilize → widen with proof → scale — always with **matched demand/supply** and **trust** in range.
